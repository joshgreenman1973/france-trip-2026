St Sernin du Plain, Burgundy

A tiny village in the Cote de Beaune wine region, between Beaune (20 min north) and Chalon-sur-Saone (20 min south). Rolling vineyards, medieval villages, and excellent food — just at a Burgundy pace.

Thursday, April 2 — Arrival Day

Getting There from Orly

  • Your brother is picking you up at Orly. ~3 hr drive south via A6 to St Sernin du Plain
  • Tip: Ask to stop at an Aire (rest stop) on the A6 — kids will need it after the flight. The ones past Auxerre are decent
  • GPS: Download Burgundy region in Google Maps before you go

Food First Night Easy Dinner

You'll arrive late afternoon, jet-lagged. Options:

  • Stock up en route: Stop at a supermarket (Carrefour, Leclerc) near Beaune before the last stretch. Get bread, cheese, charcuterie, fruit, yogurt, and wine
  • Beaune restaurants: If you have energy, Le Bistro de l'Hotel or La Ciboulette in central Beaune are family-OK
Friday, April 3 — Beaune Day

Culture Kids Hospices de Beaune

Hotel-Dieu (Hospices de Beaune)

Stunning 15th-century hospital with the famous polychrome tile roof. The 11-year-old will appreciate the history; the 5-year-old will like the colorful roof and the old pharmacy room with weird jars.

Rue de l'Hotel-Dieu, 21200 Beaune

Outdoor Kids Walk the Beaune Ramparts

Beaune's old town is surrounded by medieval ramparts with a walking path. Easy 30-40 min loop. Nice for burning off energy after a flight day.

Food Beaune Market

Saturday is the big market day (Place de la Halle), but smaller markets run other days. Great for: fresh bread, local cheeses (Epoisses!), gougeres (cheese puffs — kids love these), roast chicken.

Food Lunch in Beaune

  • Le Comptoir des Tontons — Casual, local, excellent Burgundy classics. Ask for a kids portion of jambon persille
  • Caves Madeleine — Cozy wine-bar/bistro, great cheese plates
  • Boulangeries — For a quick lunch, any boulangerie will have sandwiches, quiche, and croque-monsieurs
Saturday, April 4 — Villages & Chateaux

Culture Kids Chateau de la Rochepot

Gorgeous medieval castle with colorful Burgundian tile roofs, just 15 min from St Sernin du Plain. Fairy-tale looking. Guided tours available. Kids will love the towers and drawbridge. Check if open in early April (sometimes opens at Easter).

21340 La Rochepot (5 km west of St Sernin)

Outdoor Kids Voie Verte (Greenway) Cycling

A flat, paved, car-free cycling/walking path runs along the old canal from Beaune toward Santenay and beyond. Perfect for kids — no hills, no traffic. Rent bikes in Beaune or Santenay. The 5-year-old can ride in a trailer or tag-along.

Culture Wine Tasting (Adults)

Many domaines offer tastings. Kid-friendly approach: visit a domaine with grounds where kids can play outside while you taste. Options near you:

  • Chateau de Santenay — Has grounds, kids can explore (10 min drive)
  • Chateau de Meursault — Beautiful cellars, grounds with space for kids

Food Dinner Ideas

  • Auberge du Vieux Vigneron, Corpeau — Classic Burgundian auberge, ~10 min drive. Coq au vin, boeuf bourguignon
  • Le Montrachet, Puligny-Montrachet — More upscale but not stuffy. 10 min drive. One of the great wine villages
  • Cook at home — The supermarkets and markets will have amazing ingredients. A rotisserie chicken + salad + good wine is a perfect low-key night
Sunday, April 5 — Slower Day

Outdoor Kids Falaise de Cormot Hike

A gentle hike to limestone cliffs with panoramic views of the valley. About 25 min drive south from St Sernin. Trail is manageable for a 5-year-old (bring a carrier just in case). Roughly 1-1.5 hrs round trip.

Culture Kids Nolay

Charming medieval village 15 min west. Has the old wooden market hall (Les Halles, 14th century), a few cafes, and a quiet feel. Good for an ice cream stop and wandering. The 11-year-old might like the old stone architecture.

Kids Parc de l'Auxois

Animal park + amusement rides, about 45 min north toward Dijon. Has animals, small rides, mini-golf. Good if kids need a purely fun day. Best for the 5-year-old; the 11-year-old may find some rides young for her.

Lieu-dit Les Tilles, 21140 Arnay-sous-Vitteaux

Paris with Kids

4 days in Paris (arriving Apr 6, departing Apr 10 evening). The key with kids is: one big thing per day, leave room for playgrounds and wandering. Don't over-schedule.

Outdoor Kids Settle In + Neighborhood Walk

After checking in at Maison Breguet, explore the neighborhood on foot. Walk up to Place des Vosges (10 min) — Paris's oldest planned square, stunning arcades, and a great playground. Then wander through the Marais. Rue de la Roquette and Rue de Charonne are full of boulangeries, cafes, and shops.

Food Dinner — Night 1 (Mon Apr 6)

You'll be arriving from the train — keep it easy and close to the hotel:

Tuesday, April 7 — Classic Paris

Culture Kids Musee d'Orsay

Better than the Louvre with kids: smaller, more manageable, and the Impressionist galleries are colorful and engaging. The building itself (old train station) is impressive. The big clock windows are a hit. Plan ~2 hours.

1 Rue de la Legion d'Honneur, 75007

  • Tip: Book timed tickets online in advance. Kids under 18 are free
  • Strategy: Go in the morning when it opens. Hit the Impressionists (level 5) first

75006 Paris (RER Luxembourg or Metro Odeon)

Your Paris Notes

Shops & Fun for the Kids

Toy stores, bookshops, and the Paris Zoo — things to scatter across your days or save for when you need a win.

Kids Outdoor Paris Zoo (Parc Zoologique de Paris)

A modern zoo in the Bois de Vincennes with 5 biozones (Sahel-Sudan, Patagonia, Europe, Guyana, Madagascar). The big rock formation in the center is the landmark. Plan at least 2 hours — it's spacious and well-designed. Both kids will love it.

Avenue Daumesnil, 75012 Paris

  • Hours: Open daily. 10:00 AM - 5:00 PM (spring hours may extend — check parczoologiquedeparis.fr)
  • Price: Family pass (2 adults + 2 kids) = 68 EUR. Adults 22 EUR, kids 3-12 = 17 EUR. Under 3 free
  • Getting there from hotel: Metro Line 8 from Bastille → Porte Doree (6 stops, ~15 min). Then 10-min walk to entrance
  • Tip: Book tickets online to skip the line. There's a cafe inside but bring snacks too
  • Combine with: The Bois de Vincennes park itself is enormous — playgrounds, a lake with rowboats, and the Parc Floral (botanical garden with free playground)

Essential Money & Tipping

  • Currency: Euro (EUR). As of early 2026, roughly 1 USD = 0.90-0.95 EUR
  • Cards: Visa/Mastercard widely accepted. Amex less so. Always have some cash for markets and small cafes
  • ATMs: Use bank ATMs (not Euronet or tourist ATMs which charge high fees). Withdraw at BNP, Credit Agricole, Societe Generale
  • Tipping: Service is included in France (service compris). Rounding up or leaving 1-2 EUR for good service is appreciated but not expected
  • Tax-free shopping: Available on purchases over 100 EUR at participating stores. Ask for a detaxe form

Essential Phone & Internet

  • eSIM: Cheapest option. Get an Orange, Free, or Airalo eSIM before departure. ~10-20 EUR for 10 days of data
  • T-Mobile: If you have T-Mobile, international roaming is included (slower speeds but functional)
  • WiFi: Hotels and most cafes have WiFi. Rural Burgundy may have spottier coverage
  • Offline maps: Download Google Maps for Burgundy and Paris before leaving

Transport Car Services with Car Seats

Regular Paris taxis do NOT carry car seats. You must book a family car service in advance. Your 5-year-old needs a booster seat by French law.

  • Lajoieway — From ~50 EUR (Orly), ~65 EUR (CDG). Free car seat included (infant, toddler, or booster). Mercedes vehicles. Book at lajoieway.com
  • Family Transport Paris — Mercedes Classe V, fits up to 3 car seats at once. Airport transfers + city trips. Book at familytransportparis.com
  • Taxi Bambino — Airport transfers and city rides with car seats. Specializes in families. Book at taxibambino.com
  • G7 Famille — Paris's biggest taxi company offers "G7 Family" with baby seats + boosters. Small fleet though — book early. g7.fr
  • Taxi Baby Seat — Minivans + estate cars for 1-8 passengers, all with child seats. taxibabyseat.com

Tip: Book your Orly departure transfer (Apr 10) now — these services fill up. You'll need a booster for the 5-year-old at minimum.

Transport Paris Metro Tips

  • Navigo Easy card: Reloadable transit card, buy at any metro station. Load t+ tickets (single rides, ~2.15 EUR each)
  • Kids under 4: Free on all transit
  • Kids 4-9: Half-price tickets (your 5-year-old qualifies)
  • Kids 10+: Full price (your 11-year-old needs a regular ticket)
  • Carnet of 10: Buy 10 tickets at once for a discount
  • Strollers/buggies: Many metro stations lack elevators. RER and bus are more accessible
  • Safety: Watch for pickpockets, especially on Line 1, at Chatelet, and on the RER B to/from airports

Food Eating with Kids in France

  • Meal times: Lunch 12-2 PM, dinner 7:30-9 PM. Restaurants close between meals. Eat when the French eat or you'll find kitchens closed
  • Menus enfants: Most restaurants have kids menus (usually steak hache/hamburger, chicken, or pasta + dessert, ~8-12 EUR)
  • Boulangeries: Your best friend. Open early, have pastries, sandwiches, quiche. Pain au chocolat is a 5-year-old's dream breakfast
  • Supermarkets: Monoprix (city), Carrefour, Leclerc. Good for snacks, yogurt, fruit
  • Water: Tap water is safe everywhere. Ask for "une carafe d'eau" (free) instead of buying bottled
  • Bread: Comes with every meal, free. Don't fill up on it before the food arrives (easier said than done)
