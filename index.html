Includes: Early access to Louvre with direct access to the Mona Lisa + Seine River Cruise

Getting there: Metro Line 1 from Bastille → Palais Royal-Musee du Louvre (4 stops, ~10 min). Arrive by 9:15 AM.

Tips with kids: Don't try to see everything. Pick 2-3 highlights: Winged Victory (dramatic staircase), Egyptian mummies, Napoleon III apartments. Plan ~2-3 hours max.

Included with Louvre tickets. After the museum, take a break and hop on the boat (~1 hr). See Notre-Dame, the Eiffel Tower, and the bridges without walking. The 5-year-old will love being on a boat.

  • Currency: Euro (EUR). ~1 USD = 0.90-0.95 EUR
  • Cards: Visa/Mastercard widely accepted. Amex less so. Have some cash for markets and small cafes
  • ATMs: Use bank ATMs (BNP, Credit Agricole, Societe Generale). Avoid Euronet or tourist ATMs
  • Tipping: Service is included (service compris). Rounding up or leaving 1-2 EUR is appreciated but not expected
  • Tax-free shopping: Available on purchases over 100 EUR. Ask for a detaxe form
  • eSIM: Cheapest option. Orange, Free, or Airalo. ~10-20 EUR for 10 days of data
  • T-Mobile: International roaming included (slower speeds but functional)
  • WiFi: Hotels and most cafes. Rural Burgundy may be spottier
  • Offline maps: Download Google Maps for Burgundy and Paris before leaving

Regular Paris taxis do NOT carry car seats. Book a family car service in advance. Your 5-year-old needs a booster seat by French law.

  • Lajoieway — From ~50 EUR (Orly), ~65 EUR (CDG). Free car seat included. lajoieway.com
  • Family Transport Paris — Mercedes Classe V, fits up to 3 car seats. familytransportparis.com
  • Taxi Bambino — Specializes in families. taxibambino.com
  • G7 Famille — Paris's biggest taxi company. "G7 Family" with baby seats + boosters. Small fleet, book early. g7.fr
  • Taxi Baby Seat — Minivans + estate cars, 1-8 passengers. taxibabyseat.com

Tip: Book your Orly departure transfer (Apr 10) now — these fill up.

  • Navigo Easy card: Reloadable transit card. Load t+ tickets (~2.15 EUR each)
  • Kids under 4: Free on all transit
  • Kids 4-9: Half-price (your 5-year-old qualifies)
  • Kids 10+: Full price (your 11-year-old needs a regular ticket)
  • Carnet of 10: Buy 10 tickets at once for a discount
  • Strollers: Many stations lack elevators. RER and bus are more accessible
  • Safety: Watch for pickpockets on Line 1, at Chatelet, and on RER B
  • Meal times: Lunch 12-2 PM, dinner 7:30-9 PM. Kitchens close between meals
  • Menus enfants: Most restaurants have kids menus (~8-12 EUR)
  • Boulangeries: Your best friend. Open early, pastries, sandwiches, quiche. Pain au chocolat = dream breakfast
  • Supermarkets: Monoprix, Carrefour, Leclerc. Good for snacks, yogurt, fruit
  • Water: Tap water is safe. Ask for "une carafe d'eau" (free)
  • Bread: Comes with every meal, free. Don't fill up before the food arrives
  • Temperature: Highs 12-16C (54-61F), lows 5-8C (41-46F)
  • Rain: Moderately rainy. Expect a few wet days. Bring layers and rain jacket
  • Sun: Sunset ~8:30 PM. Long evenings!
  • Dress: Layers. T-shirt + sweater + jacket. Comfortable walking shoes essential
